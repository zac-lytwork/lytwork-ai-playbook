You can use Claude straight from your browser at claude.ai — no install needed. But if you want to get the most out of it, the desktop app is where the real power is. It's the only version that supports MCP connectors, lets Claude access your local files, and unlocks the full automation stack covered in this playbook.

🌐

Web (claude.ai)

Great for getting started and everyday chat. Access from any browser, no install needed. Doesn't support MCP connectors or local file access.

🖥️

Desktop App Recommended

Unlocks the full Claude experience. Supports MCP connectors, local files, and full automation. Everything in this playbook assumes the desktop app.

✅
What you'll need: A computer running macOS or Windows, an email address, and about 10 minutes. No credit card required to get started on the free plan.

Create Your Claude Account

Head to claude.ai and sign up with your Google account or email address. The free plan gives you access to Claude's full capabilities — you can upgrade to Claude Pro later for higher usage limits and priority access.

04 — Projects

Give Claude Context That Sticks

Projects are persistent workspaces inside Claude. Instead of re-explaining your business every time you start a chat, a Project remembers your context, tone, and instructions — so Claude always knows who it's working for.

🧠

Persistent Memory

Every conversation inside a Project inherits your instructions. Claude knows your business name, industry, tone of voice, and preferences without you repeating them.

📂

Project Knowledge

Upload documents, brand guides, SOPs, or product info directly into a Project. Claude can reference these files in any conversation within that Project.

🔌

Connected Tools

MCP connectors (covered in Section 06) are configured per Project. Your Gmail, Drive, and Calendar connections live here — ready to use in every chat.

🗂️

Separate Contexts

Run multiple Projects for different clients, teams, or purposes. Each one is isolated — a marketing project won't bleed into a finance one.

💡
How to set one up: In Claude Desktop, click New Project in the left sidebar. Give it a name, then write your system prompt — this is your standing instruction to Claude. A good starting point: "You are an AI assistant for [Business Name], a [industry] business in [City], Australia. Always write in a [professional / friendly / direct] tone. Our main services are [X, Y, Z]." Add any files you want Claude to reference, then start chatting.

06 — MCP Connectors

Connect Claude to Your Tools

MCP (Model Context Protocol) connectors are what transform Claude from a smart assistant into an active participant in your business. Once connected, Claude can read your emails, pull up files, check your calendar, and update your systems — without you ever leaving the conversation.

How to connect your tools in Claude Desktop
1
Open Claude Desktop and go to Settings

Click your profile icon in the bottom-left corner, then select Settings. From there, navigate to the Integrations tab — this is where all your MCP connectors live.

2
Browse and add a connector

You'll see a list of available connectors. Click Add next to the one you want — for example, Google Drive. Claude will walk you through the authorisation flow for that service.

3
Authorise access

Each connector asks for specific permissions — for example, Gmail asks to read and send emails on your behalf. You can review exactly what Claude will and won't have access to before confirming. Claude only accesses what you've explicitly allowed.

🔒 A note on permissions: Claude operates with the minimum access needed. For Cowork and Code, permissions extend to local files and system actions — always review what a connector is requesting before authorising, especially for technical integrations.
4
Test it in a conversation

Once connected, start a new chat and try it: "Check my Gmail for any unread emails from the last 24 hours" or "Find the proposal doc in my Drive." Claude will confirm the connection is working and pull the results directly into your chat.

🔌
Start here: Gmail and Google Drive are the highest-value, lowest-effort connectors available. Most Australian businesses are already on Google Workspace — get these two working first and you'll immediately understand why MCP changes everything.

08 — Skills

Supercharge Claude with Skills

Skills are pre-built capability bundles that extend what Claude can do. Think of them like apps on your phone — each one gives Claude specialised knowledge and tools for a specific type of task.

How Skills Work

Skills work inside Claude Cowork and Claude Code. When you invoke a skill — for example, the PPTX skill — Claude gains detailed knowledge of how to create a perfect PowerPoint presentation, what tools to use, and how to format the output professionally. Without the skill, Claude would give you a basic result. With the skill, it produces something polished and ready to use.


Skills come bundled with Claude Cowork and can also be installed via plugins — shareable bundles of skills, MCP connectors, and tools that extend Claude's capabilities for specific industries or use cases.
